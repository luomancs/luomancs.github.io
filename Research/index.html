Assistant Intern, Research &Development Department

The Project I joined is developing a data analyzing platform of Medicare data of Beijing. Accumulating for decades, the Medicare data is over millions, which impedes working staff to check relating data. This project aims for developing a system to satisfy the demands of checking, summarizing, analyzing Medicare data, supervising businesses and assisting in enacting Medicare policies. The project could be divided into following models: distracting, processing, distributed-memorizing, distributed-calculating, modeling, visualizing Medicare data.
I took part in developing system of processing data, using the SPARK distributed-calculating technique to process Medicare date and select the useful data of business. My task was figuring out the outlets of patients and physicians. During this period, I applied Aprior and Elcat algorithms to generate rules which could discover the fraud of people.

Mathematical Contest in Modeling

In January of 2017, I worked with two students to take part in Mathematical Contest in Modeling of the United States. Our task was to investigate how to realize intelligent urbanization. Two cities were chosen, Yushu and Nottingham. We obtained their city developing state from the google map and digitalized the remote sensing image. We employed principal component analysis approach to select ten indicators which could best predict the development of the city. We modeled with ten formulas to calculate each indicator. The final mathematic model was a combination of ten indicators with different weight. We collected the data of Shenzhen City from 1990 to 2000, in order to simulate ten-year development path and compare with the actual situations. The results showed that the deviation was less than 10%. Our valid and stable outcome was approval by official persons that in the end, our team was awarded honorable mention title. Here you could review the paper.

2016

Speech Recognition With Weighted Finite-State Transducers(WFST)

I took part in a project, with the aim of analyszing huge amount of data requiring small storied-space. The main task of this project was cutting-off the original data into smaller pieces, after analysing, the results can be combined together. In this process, I mainly studied the WFST, including algorithms for composition for combining models, weighted determinization and minimization for optimizing time and space re-quirements, and a weight pushing algorithm for re-distributing transition weights optimally for speech recognition. Before participating in this project, I learned many basic techniques such as GMM, HMM, HLDA, LDA, PCA, BP etc. Also I used the open source Kaldi to test speech data.
