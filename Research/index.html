Research

2017

Manuscript Simulation Based on RNNs

The main purpose of this research is predicting and simulating different styles of handwriting. My main task consists of three parts: learning RNNs, LSTM models, Mixture density networks, Mixture of bivariate Gaussians and Bernoulli distributions; building RNNLIB environment on remote server and running through the experiments; adjusting the parameters and trained the model with online handwriting data from IAM-OnDB.

Data Processing & Analyzing Platform for Medicare data in Beijing

The Project I participated in is developing a Data Processing & Analyzing Platform for Medicare data in Beijing. There are more than 20 million patients and nearly 5 billion medical treatment data collected during last decade, which is too enormous for working staff to manipulate these data. This project is aimed at developing a platform to satisfy the demands of checking, summarizing, analyzing these data, and supervising the enactment of Medicare policies. The project could be divided into the four major parts: extracting current data from database into Hadoop based Big Data platform, predictive modeling, Spark based distributed-processing, and data visualizing.

I was instructed to develop a model which can indicate possible fraudulent pattern. with Apriori algorithm. After adopting Apriori and reading some learning materials, I supposed that Eclat algorithm might have higher efficiency. So I applied rule based on Apriori and Eclat algorithms to generate the model, implementing this model in Scala, and running program in Spark processing platform. After several trials, the model I developed got a confidence about 80%, which was above the predetermined 75% threshold.

Experimental Course Management System

I took part in a project entitled Online Course Experiment System from April 2017 to June 2017, in which I acquired the ability of developing systems, programming in Java. Students could select experiment, submit their solution to subjects, also inquiry answers. Teachers could publish experiments, subjects, and answers. The specialty of this system is automatically scoring students’ responses of experiences by Regular Expression.

Mathematical Contest in Modeling

In January of 2017, I worked with two students to take part in Mathematical Contest in Modeling of the United States. Our task was to investigate how to realize smart growth city. We finally complete to build models in linear regression approach, predicating the development of cities and offering smart growth plans for cities.
During the contest, two cities were chosen, Yushu and Nottingham. We obtained their city developing situation from the google map and create the accurate data by ArcGIS software. We summarize nine factors and formulate ten indicators. We employed principal component analysis approach to weight each indicator. The final mathematic model was a combination of ten indicators with different weight. We collected the data of Shenzhen City from 1990 to 2000, in order to simulate ten-year development path and compare with the actual situations. The results showed that the deviation was less than 10%.

2016

Speech Recognition With Weighted Finite-State Transducers

I took part in a project, with the aim of analyzing huge amount of data requiring small storied-space. The main task of this project was cutting-off the original data into smaller pieces, after analyzing, the results can be combined together. In this process, I mainly studied the WFST, including algorithms for composition for combining models, weighted determination and minimization for optimizing time and space requirements, and a weight pushing algorithm for re-distributing transition weights optimally for speech recognition. Before participating in this project, I learned many basic techniques such as GMM, HMM, HLDA, LDA, PCA, BP etc. Also I used the open source Kaldi to test speech data.
